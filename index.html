Workshop submitted to ACII 2019, Cambridge UK
Emotion in Groups
ABSTRACT

Although a major goal of the area of affective computing, the study of affect in groups has received relatively less attention compared to individual models of affect. As an increasing number of technologies modeling affect move into the wild, it is important to realize that sensing affect in isolation does not always reflect the emotional dynamics resulting from interaction in groups. This is particularly the case for Human Computer Interaction (HCI) systems that deal with multiparty conversations or that need to estimate overall emotion from a crowd.

Such applications rely on a diverse set of expertise ranging from multimodal sensing and dialogue modeling to behavioral signal processing and machine learning. The multi-disciplinary nature of the ACII conference makes this an ideal venue for a workshop on this topic.

The workshop will provide an opportunity for researchers working on emotion in groups to showcase their current research, discuss the challenges and how to best address them, foster collaborations, and steer research efforts and interest towards this important problem.
